Vegetarian Shepards Pie

A winter warming dish packed with vegetables. This dish freezes well, if your freezer is tight for space you can freeze the lentil veg mix and mash separate and reconstruct when defrosted.

Ingredients
Serves 2
  • 1 large carrot
  • 1 stick of celery
  • 1 white onion
  • 150g mushrooms
  • 200g red lentils
  • 100g peas
  • Tomato puree
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • 1 cup of red wine
  • 900ml vegetable stock
  • 50g butter
  • 500g potatoes- used a mix of sweet and normal
  • 60g of cheddar grated
Instructions

Peel and cut the potatoes into equal sizes. Boil the potatoes in a pan for 10 minutes until potatoes are soft and breaking apart. Drain the potatoes keeping a mug of the potato water. Return the potatoes to the pan with a splash of the water to start, a glug of olive oil and the butter. Mash the potatoes, adding more water if needed and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Dice onion and celery and half of the carrot. Cut the other half into half moons. Add to a pan with oil and fry for about 10 mins until soft.

Roughly chop the mushrooms and add to the pan, continue to fry for 5 minutes. Add approximately two tbsp of tomato puree to the pan and fry for 2 minutes until it begins to catch on the pan.

Deglaze the pan with the wine or stock if you aren't using wine. Let the alcohol cook off for a few minutes then add the stock, the lentils and the thyme. Allow to simmer for 20/25 minutes until the lentil sauce has slightly reduced down.

Put the lentil mixture into an overproof dish, remove the thyme and top with the mash. Spread the mash over with a fork so it's textured and top with the cheese. Place under the grill for about ten minutes until the cheese has melted and it's crispy on top.
