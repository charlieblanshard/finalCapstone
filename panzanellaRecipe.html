Panzanella Salad

Panzanella is a Tuscan salad which uses stale bread and ripe summer tomatoes. Usually enjoyed with mozzerella but feta makes a lovely twist. I'd be happy if I was served this dish in a resturant it's so good and it's always gone down a treat with others.

Ingredients
Serves 2
  • Butternut Squash 300g
  • 1 Bell Pepper
  • 1 bunch Flat Leaf Parsley
  • 1 Ciabatta Roll
  • 1 bag of Rocket
  • 1 Red Onion
  • 1 large Garlic Clove
  • 1 punnet Plum Tomatoes
  • 1/2 Lemon
  • 100g Feta Cheese
  • 2 Tablespoons of Capers
  • Chilli Flakes- to taste
Instructions

Preheat the oven to 200¬∞C. Dice butternut squashand place on a large baking tray, drizzle with a little oil and season with salt and pepper and mix to coat the butternut. Roast on the top shelf of the oven for 25-30 mins in total until soft.

Halve the tomatoes. When the squash has been in the oven for 10 mins, turn the squash and add the tomatoes to the tray with another drizzle of oil if needed. Return to the oven for the remainder of the cooking time.

Peel and thinly slice the onion. Halve the pepper and slice into thin strips. Heat a splash of oil in a large frying pan over a medium-low heat and add the onion and pepper to the pan. Cook until the peppers and onion are really soft approx 10-15mins.

Tear the ciabatta into small, bite-size pieces. Put the ciabatta on another baking tray with a drizzle of oil and a pinch of salt and mix. Bake in the the oven until golden and crispy, 8-10 mins. Give the tray a shake halfway through so the bread does not catch.

Zest then halve the lemon and dice the capers. Make the dressing by combining the olive oil (about 2 tbs), parsley, capers, lemon zest and half the lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper, mix well and set aside.

When the peppers are soft, stir in the garlic and cook for a minute more. Remove from the heat and add to the bowl with the dressing. Once ready, remove the veggies and croutons from the oven, add to the bowl and gently mix. Let the bowl sit for a couple minutes so everything can absorb! Add the rocket then divide between bowls and crumble the feta over the top.
